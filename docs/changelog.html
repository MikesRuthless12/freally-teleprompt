0.200.0

21 July 2026 — the standalone teleprompter

Scripts you can keep, a projector for the talent, a real transport, and a prompter that reads itself aloud.

Added

  • A script library. Create, open, rename and delete scripts, with a recent list and autosave while you type. They are saved as plain text files, so a script of yours stays readable in any editor — even if this app disappears tomorrow. The app reopens whatever you had open last.
  • Pause chips in the editor. Every inline -- pause shows as a small pill with its length. It behaves as one thing: the cursor steps over it, Backspace removes the whole pause in one press, and copying it gives you back real -- text. Type two dashes and one appears.
  • A projector window for the talent. Put the reading surface on any connected display, filling it edge to edge, with a mirror flip for beam-splitter prompter glass. It scrolls off the same state as your preview, so the two can never disagree, and it has its own transport and keyboard shortcuts for whoever is reading.
  • Mirror to a phone or tablet. Show the scrolling script on any device on your own network, from a link and QR code the app gives you — each device picks its own font size without changing what anyone else sees. It is off by default, stays on this computer until you explicitly open it to the network, and is read-only: nothing on a phone can start, stop, or edit anything.
  • A proper transport. Play, pause, stop, rewind and fast-forward as real buttons that repeat when held; a seek bar showing elapsed and total reading time, with a preview of the words at any point; click a word to start there; and mousewheel scrubbing.
  • Speed in BPM. Read the pace as characters per second, or as BPM (20–250) if you are pacing to music.
  • Appearance controls. Typeface, weight, text colour, side margins, line spacing, and where the reading guide sits on screen — applied to every surface at once.
  • Read aloud. Have the prompter read your script at the pace you have set, to check timing before a take. Pauses are real silences, and the highlight follows the spoken word. It uses the voices your computer already has — nothing is downloaded — and it never moves the talent's projector.

Changed

  • The app draws its own window. A centred title, with Settings, About and the minimise / maximise / close buttons on the right — matching the other Freally apps. It still drags, double-clicks to maximise, and resizes from any edge or corner.
  • A proper Settings dialog, with a category sidebar (General, Reading, Appearance, Projector, Network), a search box, and OK / Cancel / Apply.
  • Minimize to the system tray, off by default. With it on, minimising tucks the window into the tray; restoring brings it back and removes the tray icon, so it is never in both places at once.
  • The app icon is now the same artwork at every size. Small icons used to show a cropped "F" instead; one consistent mark reads better in the taskbar.

Still no AI, no accounts, no telemetry. The network mirror serves your script to devices you point at it, on your own network. Nothing is uploaded and nothing is relayed through anyone's server.

0.100.0

21 July 2026 — the scaffold phase

The app now builds, runs, and gates on the agreement, with the character-based scroll engine ported over from Freally Capture.

Added

  • The character-based teleprompter engine. Scroll position is a real reading pace measured in characters per second, with inline -- pauses (--2 holds for two seconds), seek and step controls, and an optional countdown before the scroll begins. The preview and the projector lay text on one fixed-width stage, so they wrap at the identical column whatever the window size.
  • Single-window app shell with a script box and the live prompter surface, plus Settings that only take effect when you press Apply — cancelling restores exactly what you had.
  • 18 languages, following your system language by default.
  • First-run agreement. The app asks once, remembers your acceptance, and never loses it when you save settings.
  • Problem reporter and update check. If the app ever stops unexpectedly it writes a report locally and offers to send it — by GitHub issue, Gmail, or your own mail app. Nothing is ever sent without your click, and you read the exact text first.
  • App icons for Windows, macOS and Linux. Small sizes use a glyph-only "F", because the full wordmark is unreadable below 64px.

No AI, no accounts, no telemetry. The only request Freally Teleprompt makes is the update check, and it sends nothing about you, your machine, or your scripts.

Installers are not published yet

0.200.0 is a development milestone — the release pipeline lands in a later phase. When the first installers are cut they will appear here for Windows, macOS, and Linux — free, local-first, no account. Meanwhile, the documentation describes what the app does.
