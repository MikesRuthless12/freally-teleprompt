Mirror a script on your phone, tablet, or a second laptop

A running copy of Freally Teleprompt can show its scrolling script to any device on the same network — in a browser, with nothing to install and no account.

How to join

  • On the computer running the app: open Settings → Mirror to my network, tick Mirror the script to devices on my network, then tick Allow other devices, not just this computer, and press Apply.
  • A link and a QR code appear. That link is the page — it is served by the app itself, on your own network.
  • On the phone: scan the code, or type the link into any browser. Make sure the phone is on the same Wi-Fi.
  • Set your own text size with the A− / A+ buttons. It changes only your screen — the operator's preview and the talent's projector are untouched.

Why the link is not on this website

Because it cannot be. This page is served over HTTPS from the public site, and browsers deliberately block an HTTPS page from talking to a plain-HTTP device on your local network. So the mirror page is served by the app, directly to your device, and the app hands you its address. That is also the more private arrangement: your script never travels through this website at all.

If it does not connect

  • Check both switches. "Allow other devices" is separate from turning the mirror on, on purpose — with it off, the mirror only answers on the computer itself.
  • Same network. A phone on mobile data, or on a guest network that isolates clients, cannot reach the computer.
  • Firewall. The first time, Windows or macOS may ask whether to allow incoming connections. Say yes for private networks.
  • Port already in use. If Settings shows a red "could not start" line, change the port and press Apply again.

Your script never leaves your network, and the mirror cannot touch anything. The page is served by the desktop app straight to devices you point at it — nothing is uploaded and nothing is relayed through a server. It is read-only: no browser can start, stop, seek, or edit. The link carries a key that changes every time the app launches, and because it is plain HTTP on your own network, use it on a network you trust. And, as everywhere in Freally Teleprompt, there is no AI involved.
